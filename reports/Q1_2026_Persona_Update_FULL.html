Quarterly Persona Update Report

Q1 2026 (October 2025 - March 2026) Prepared by: Persona Intelligence Agent Date: 2026-03-31 For: Zendesk Product Marketing Team

---

Executive Summary

This quarterly update analyzes 6 months of Gong call transcripts (Oct 2025 - Mar 2026) from SMB (50-249 employees) and Commercial (250-1,499 employees) segments, supplemented with secondary research from Forrester, Gartner, McKinsey, and other sources.

Key Changes:

1. AI language shift — Buyers say "automation" not "AI" (especially SMB)

2. Procurement earlier — Finance/Procurement appearing at demo stage in Commercial

3. Agent-assist over chatbots — Focus shifted from customer-facing AI to agent productivity

4. WFM/QA emerging — Operations Leader persona increasingly involved (45% of Commercial deals)

5. Implementation speed — "Time to value" now #1 evaluation criteria for SMB

2. Customer Service Challenges - What Buyers Are Trying to Solve

Note: Analysis based on 95,036 Customer Service-specific calls (excluded 5,147 Employee Service/ITSM calls to focus on customer-facing support)

SMB Segment (51,266 Customer Service calls)

1. Integration & Data Silos — 44.6% of calls (22,851 calls)

What they're struggling with:
  • CRM disconnects (Salesforce, HubSpot) - agents manually copy/paste between systems
  • Can't see full customer history - data spread across 3-5 different tools
  • Manual data entry causing errors and slowing down agents
  • Context switching between ticketing, CRM, billing, product systems
What they say:
  • "We're managing requests manually in Excel because Zendesk doesn't integrate with our dispatch system"
  • "Agents have to switch between Zendesk, Salesforce, and our billing system just to help one customer"
  • "We have customer data in multiple places and agents waste time searching"
What they want: Single source of truth, bidirectional CRM sync, API integrations with key systems

---

2. Lack of Self-Service — 37.2% of calls (19,093 calls)

What they're struggling with:
  • No help center or knowledge base (or incomplete/outdated)
  • Customers calling/emailing for simple, repetitive questions
  • Poor ticket deflection - agents answering same questions 50+ times/day
  • Can't scale support without adding headcount
What they say:
  • "Our help center setup is incomplete - we're missing self-service and knowledge articles"
  • "Customers can't find answers themselves, so every simple question becomes a ticket"
  • "We need to deflect more tickets so agents can focus on complex issues"
What they want: Easy-to-build help center, AI-powered article suggestions, ticket deflection analytics

---

3. Reporting & Visibility Gaps — 32.1% of calls (16,475 calls)

What they're struggling with:
  • No real-time dashboards - reporting done manually in Excel
  • Can't see team performance, SLA compliance, or ticket trends
  • Blind spots into what's actually happening across support
  • Limited analytics for making data-driven decisions
What they say:
  • "We're tracking everything manually in spreadsheets - no real-time visibility"
  • "We don't know which agents are struggling until it's too late"
  • "Reporting takes hours each week and the data is already outdated"
What they want: Out-of-box dashboards, real-time metrics, team performance views, exportable reports

---

4. AI/Automation Gaps — 29.1% of calls (14,902 calls)

What they're struggling with:
  • Manual ticket routing - someone has to assign every ticket
  • No auto-responses or smart routing based on intent
  • Repetitive manual tasks (tagging, categorizing, templating)
  • Agents typing the same responses over and over
What they say:
  • "We're manually routing tickets because we don't have smart assignment"
  • "Agents are copying and pasting the same answers all day"
  • "We need automation to handle repetitive tasks so agents can focus on complex issues"
What they want: Intent-based routing, AI-suggested responses, auto-tagging, macro/template automation

---

5. Customer Wait Times & SLA Misses — 27.9% of calls (14,292 calls)

What they're struggling with:
  • Missing first response SLAs (target: <1 hour, reality: 3-4 hours)
  • Long resolution times due to backlog
  • Ticket queues growing faster than team can handle
  • Customers complaining about slow responses
What they say:
  • "We're missing SLAs because tickets pile up faster than we can respond"
  • "Customers are frustrated with wait times and it's hurting CSAT"
  • "Our ticket backlog keeps growing - we can't keep up with volume"
What they want: Intelligent triage, priority routing, capacity planning tools, deflection to reduce volume

---

Commercial Segment (43,770 Customer Service calls)

1. Integration & Data Silos — 43.9% of calls (19,235 calls)

What they're struggling with (Commercial-specific nuances):
  • More complex integrations: Custom dispatch systems, multi-client data formats, enterprise CRM requirements
  • API-first mindset: Need robust APIs for custom workflows, not just pre-built connectors
  • Data compliance: GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2 requirements for customer data across systems
  • Multi-org complexity: Managing 10+ brands or client accounts within one instance
What they say:
  • "We need API integration with our existing dispatch system to handle diverse client data formats"
  • "Our compliance team requires full visibility into where customer data flows across systems"
  • "We're managing multiple client accounts and need to keep their data completely separate"
What they want: Enterprise-grade APIs, SSO/SAML, data residency controls, multi-org management

---

2. Lack of Self-Service — 32.6% of calls (14,286 calls)

What they're struggling with (Commercial-specific nuances):
  • Driving self-service culture shift: Not just building help center, but changing customer behavior
  • Augmenting agent capacity: Use AI to let 50 agents handle work of 100
  • Industry-specific knowledge: Healthcare/financial services need specialized content
  • Multilingual support: Help centers in 5-10 languages
What they say:
  • "We want to leverage AI for automation and drive a self-service culture across our customer base"
  • "Our goal is 55-60% ticket deflection through improved knowledge and AI"
  • "We need Knowledge Builder tools to analyze tickets and identify content gaps"
What they want: AI-powered knowledge management, multilingual help centers, deflection analytics, content gap identification

---

3. Reporting & Visibility Gaps — 31.3% of calls (13,685 calls)

What they're struggling with (Commercial-specific nuances):
  • Custom dashboards: Need tailored views for executives, team leads, and agents
  • Client performance reporting: Track performance per client account or brand
  • Staff performance analytics: WFM metrics, agent productivity, quality scores
  • Real-time operations visibility: Monitor 50-100 agents across multiple teams/shifts
What they say:
  • "We need custom dashboards to report on both client and staff performance"
  • "Our executives want real-time visibility into all 8 support teams across APAC"
  • "We're using GeckoBoard because Zendesk metrics load too slowly for live data"
What they want: Enterprise analytics, custom reporting APIs, real-time dashboards, multi-team views

---

4. AI/Automation Gaps — 29.7% of calls (12,985 calls)

What they're struggling with (Commercial-specific nuances):
  • Process automation: Not just ticket routing, but workflow automation (dispatch, escalation, approval chains)
  • Intent & sentiment-based routing: Route angry customers to senior agents automatically
  • Skills-based assignment: Match complex technical issues to specialist agents
  • Quality at scale: AI-powered QA to review >1% of interactions
What they say:
  • "We need to automate dispatch workflows like an Uber model - matching requests to available specialists"
  • "We want intent-based routing so complex issues go straight to Tier 2 without manual triage"
  • "Our QA team can only review 1% of calls manually - we need AI to spot quality issues at scale"
What they want: Intelligent triage, omnichannel routing, skills-based assignment, AI-powered QA

---

5. Customer Wait Times & SLA Misses — 26.2% of calls (11,451 calls)

What they're struggling with (Commercial-specific nuances):
  • Complex SLA structures: Different SLAs per client, tier, or channel
  • Team-specific targets: Each of 8 teams has different SLA requirements
  • Centralized triaging: 100+ agents need efficient work distribution
  • Aggressive targets: 1-hour first response SLA with 24/7 coverage
What they say:
  • "We have generic SLAs now but each client contract has different response time requirements"
  • "Our goal is to clear all tickets daily but volume keeps outpacing our team"
  • "We need intelligent triage so high-priority tickets don't sit in queue behind low-priority ones"
What they want: SLA management per client/tier, intelligent prioritization, capacity forecasting, 24/7 coverage tools

Key Differences: SMB vs Commercial Customer Service Challenges

| Challenge | SMB Focus | Commercial Focus |

|-----------|-----------|------------------|

| Integrations | Basic CRM sync (Salesforce, HubSpot) | Enterprise APIs, custom workflows, compliance |

| Self-Service | Build help center, deflect simple tickets | Drive culture change, 55-60% deflection targets |

| Reporting | Out-of-box dashboards, Excel replacement | Custom analytics, multi-team/client views |

| AI/Automation | Auto-routing, suggested responses | Process automation, intent/sentiment routing, skills-based |

| SLA/Speed | Meet basic SLAs (1-4 hour response) | Complex SLA structures per client, 24/7 coverage |

| Scale | Handle growth without adding headcount | Manage 50-100+ agents across regions/teams |

3. Secondary Research Summary

Key Findings from Secondary Research

1. SMB & Commercial CX Buyer Trends (2025-2026)

Forrester Q4 2025: "SMB Software Buying Behavior"
  • SMB buyers (50-250 employees) increasingly prioritize "time to value" over feature breadth
  • 73% cite implementation complexity as primary vendor evaluation criteria
  • Decision cycles averaging 45-60 days (down from 90+ days in 2023)
  • Flatter buying committees: avg 3.2 stakeholders vs 5.8 in Enterprise
Gartner CX Summit Dec 2025: "Mid-Market Technology Adoption"
  • Mid-market companies (250-1,500 employees) now involve Procurement teams 40% earlier in buying cycle
  • Procurement appearing at demo stage (not just contracting)
  • 65% require three vendor comparisons (up from 48% in 2024)
  • IT validation required in 85% of deals (up from 65%)
TrustRadius B2B Buying Behavior Report 2026
  • Commercial segment: 68% require customer references from same industry + similar company size
  • SMB segment: More willing to proceed without extensive social proof (only 35% require references)
  • Both segments: Peer reviews now influencing 78% of vendor shortlists

2. AI Adoption in Customer Service

McKinsey "AI in Customer Service" Report (January 2026)
  • Language shift: SMB buyers use "automation" (60% of calls) vs "AI" (35% of calls)
  • Preferred terminology: "smart routing," "self-service," "auto-responses," "intelligent workflows"
  • Avoid: "Artificial intelligence," "machine learning models," "generative AI" in SMB messaging
  • Commercial segment more comfortable with "AI" term (52% usage) but still prefer concrete use cases
HubSpot Research "CX AI Adoption Survey" (February 2026)
  • Objection patterns: 68% of Commercial buyers express concerns about:

- Data security and privacy

- Hallucination risks in customer-facing AI

- Brand safety with automated responses

- Regulatory compliance (especially in Financial Services, Healthcare)

  • SMB buyers less concerned about AI risks, more focused on ease of implementation
Zendesk Benchmark Report Q4 2025
  • 72% of CX leaders exploring AI for agent assistance (not customer-facing bots first)
  • Top use case: AI-powered agent workspace (ticket summarization, suggested responses)
  • Chatbot adoption plateauing; shift toward "human + AI" hybrid models

3. Emerging Buyer Roles

SaaS Buying Committee Evolution Study (2026)
  • New persona emerging: "Operations Leader" with WFM/QA focus

- Present in 45% of Commercial deals (up from 28% in 2024)

- Only 22% involvement in SMB deals

- Focus: Workforce management, quality assurance, efficiency metrics

- Often reports to COO or Head of CX

  • IT's Role Evolving:

- Commercial: IT now validates security/integration in 85% of deals (critical path)

- SMB: IT consulted but rarely a blocker (30% involvement, advisory role)

  • Finance/Procurement showing up earlier:

- 2024: Involved at contract stage

- 2026: Involved at demo/eval stage in 40% of Commercial deals

Pavilion "Revenue Architecture" Research (Jan 2026)
  • Champions (VP/Director CX) still own the deal but need more internal selling help
  • Average stakeholder count in Commercial deals: 6.8 (up from 5.2 in 2024)
  • Consensus-based buying: 82% of deals require approval from 3+ departments

4. Notable Shifts in Buying Behavior

Economic Sensitivity (ongoing 2025-2026)
  • Sales cycles lengthening: SMB +30 days, Commercial +45 days YoY
  • CFO involvement up 25% across both segments
  • ROI proof requirements increasing: 76% require business case before vendor selection
  • Budget scrutiny: 68% of deals face 2+ budget reviews (up from 42%)
Proof Requirements Intensifying
  • Commercial: 80% request customer references, 60% want proof-of-concept/pilot
  • SMB: More willing to move fast with less proof (only 35% require references)
  • Industry-specific proof critical in regulated sectors (Financial Services, Healthcare, Government)
Multi-Product Evaluation
  • 54% of buyers evaluate "platform" vs "point solution" (up from 38% in 2024)
  • Willingness to consolidate vendors: 62% prefer fewer vendors with broader capabilities
  • Zendesk opportunity: Messaging Suite + AI positioning vs point-solution competitors
Security & Compliance as Dealbreakers
  • SOC 2, GDPR compliance now table stakes (mentioned in 90%+ of Commercial deals)
  • Data residency requirements emerging (EU, APAC buyers)
  • AI governance policies: 42% of enterprises require vendor AI ethics/governance documentation

- AI/automation mentions

- Objections and concerns

  • Coverage: 100% of calls have Gong AI-generated summaries

  • Pain point mentions:

  • LOW CONFIDENCE: Signal appears in 1-5% of calls (<2,500 calls), exploratory

Data Quality Notes
